## Design-Prompt: DCI Data Center Intelligence
**Style: Swiss/International**

Craft a single-page website for DCI Data Center Intelligence — a German consultancy with 30 years of expertise in data center planning, consulting, project management, and certification — using the Swiss/International design style. The page should evoke an immediate sense of engineered precision and institutional trust. Visitors should feel as though they have entered a space governed by logic, clarity, and deep competence. The visual hierarchy must be built on a rigorous grid system, using DCI's brand palette of deep navy (#004E78) as the primary anchor and a confident medium blue (#207CB5) as the accent, set against generous white space. Every element should breathe — the rhythm of the page should feel like a well-organized blueprint where nothing is accidental. Incorporate colorful accents through the blue spectrum to create moments of visual interest within the systematic structure, reinforcing the brand's identity as a partner for future-proof data centers.

The typographic system should feel authoritative yet approachable — use Inter or a similar geometric sans-serif that channels the spirit of Helvetica with modern screen legibility. Headlines should carry weight and conviction, set large and confident, while body text remains measured and highly readable with generous line height. The overall user experience should feel like turning through the pages of a Swiss engineering manual: each section is a precisely bounded module within the grid, clearly delineated by whitespace rather than decorative borders. The scrolling journey should progress from an opening statement of authority and credibility (awards, 30 years of expertise), through the four core services (Beratung, Planung, Steuerung, Zertifizierung) presented as equal-weight grid modules, into social proof through industry references and a client testimonial, arriving at a clean contact section that feels like the natural conclusion of a well-structured argument. Interactions should be minimal and precise — no excess, just purposeful micro-movements that confirm the user's actions.

The aesthetic should channel the feeling of walking through a world-class research facility or a meticulously organized control room — spaces where every cable is labeled, every indicator light has meaning, and complexity has been mastered into elegant simplicity. Think of the design philosophy behind precision instruments, the clarity of technical drawings, the quiet confidence of German engineering exhibitions where sophistication is expressed through restraint rather than ornamentation. The page should feel like the architectural plans DCI themselves create: systematic, trustworthy, and undeniably competent. Reference the emotional quality of institutions that have earned respect through decades of consistent excellence — the sense that everything is exactly where it should be, that no detail has been overlooked, and that reliability is not just promised but structurally guaranteed through the design itself.
