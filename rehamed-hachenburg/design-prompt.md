## Design-Prompt: REHAMED Hachenburg
**Style: Scandinavian**

Adopt a Scandinavian design philosophy to craft a single-page website for REHAMED Hachenburg GmbH, an established outpatient rehabilitation clinic in the Westerwald region of Germany, operating since 1997 across 1,900 sqm of clinical space. The page should radiate a sense of warmth, trust, and quiet competence — the feeling of walking into a space where healing is not just promised but palpable. The visual hierarchy should guide visitors through a gentle, reassuring journey: from a welcoming hero showcasing the clinic's bright, modern facility, through the breadth of services — ambulatory rehabilitation, physiotherapy, and ergotherapy for orthopedic and trauma patients — to the multidisciplinary team and finally an inviting contact section. Ground the palette in the brand's warm soft-yellow (#FFFFAE) and deep maroon (#800000) against clean white (#FFFFFF) backgrounds, using the maroon as a confident anchor for headings and interactive elements while the yellow provides gentle warmth as an accent. Integrate colorful, nature-inspired touches that echo the Westerwald's forested landscape to enhance emotional resonance.

The typography should feel approachable yet authoritative — replace the existing Tahoma/Times New Roman stack with a modern humanist sans-serif pairing (such as Inter or Source Sans for body, paired with a refined serif like Source Serif or Lora for headings) that conveys medical professionalism without clinical coldness. Interactions and scroll transitions should feel like a slow, deep breath: smooth, organic, unhurried — micro-animations that appear as naturally as light shifting through a window. The emotional arc of the single-page scroll should progress from a welcoming embrace (hero with clinic imagery and the REHAMED logo), through confidence-building depth (services overview, team composition including physicians, sports scientists, psychologists, social workers, and nutritional experts), to practical reassurance (opening hours Monday–Thursday 7:30–19:30, Friday 7:30–15:00, address at Saynstraße 38, D-57627 Hachenburg, phone 02662/940880, and the in-house transport service), culminating in a gentle call-to-action that feels like an open hand rather than a sales push.

Draw inspiration from the atmosphere of a Scandinavian wellness retreat — the tactile warmth of light wood, the calm of uncluttered spaces, the quiet confidence of a room designed around human comfort rather than aesthetic showmanship. Think of the emotional quality found in architecture that prioritizes natural light and organic materials, the hygge philosophy where every detail exists to make people feel held and cared for. Channel the design sensibility of Nordic healthcare environments where clinical precision meets human warmth — spaces that feel neither sterile nor informal, but perfectly calibrated to put anxious patients at ease. These abstract qualities should permeate every pixel of the final design: generous whitespace that breathes, rounded corners that soften, imagery that connects to real therapeutic practice (aquatic therapy, reception, training), and a layout rhythm that mirrors the steady, restorative pace of rehabilitation itself.
