## Design-Prompt: Kruschel Heizung-Sanitär GmbH
**Style: Scandinavian**

Adopt a Scandinavian design aesthetic — rooted in hygge, natural warmth, and warm minimalism — to craft a single-page website for Kruschel Heizung-Sanitär GmbH, a master-certified heating, plumbing, and bathroom renovation company serving the Westerwald region of Germany. The moment visitors arrive, they should feel as though they've stepped into a beautifully curated, light-filled space — calm, welcoming, and unmistakably competent. The primary brand color, a soft aqua teal (#69C5D3), should serve as the guiding thread through the page, evoking water, clean energy, and modern comfort, while the fresh green accent (#75D69C) punctuates calls-to-action and sustainability messaging like a living plant in a sunlit room. The visual hierarchy should flow like a quiet conversation: the hero section presenting their three signature bathroom images in a gentle, unhurried showcase, then easing into their story of craftsmanship, sustainability, and regional expertise. Use Montserrat for headings to convey modern confidence, paired with Open Sans for body text that reads as approachable and clear. The company logo (https://kruschel.info/wp-content/uploads/2018/05/Kruschel_Logo.png) should sit naturally in the header, grounded and unpretentious. Incorporate colorful elements — soft teal washes, muted green highlights, warm off-white backgrounds — to create emotional warmth without visual clutter.

The design philosophy should channel the Scandinavian principle of "lagom" — just the right amount. Typography should feel simultaneously authoritative and approachable: Montserrat headings that speak with quiet confidence about decades of Meisterbetrieb expertise, while Open Sans body text invites homeowners into a comfortable dialogue about their heating and bathroom dreams. Interactions and scroll animations should feel organic and unhurried — elements fading gently into view like morning light filling a room, subtle parallax on the bathroom hero images that creates depth without drama. The emotional journey of this single page should progress from inspiration (stunning bathroom imagery and a clear value proposition) through trust-building (their commitment to sustainability, energy efficiency, and regional roots in Neunkhausen) to warm invitation (visiting their showroom, scheduling a consultation). The dark text (#2A2A2A) against generous white space creates the restful contrast that lets content breathe. Every section transition should feel like moving from one room of a well-designed home to the next — each space purposeful, each moment comfortable, the overall experience leaving visitors with the feeling that this is a company that truly cares about creating spaces where people feel at home.

Draw abstract inspiration from the feeling of a Nordic wellness space — the serenity of natural wood meeting white ceramic, the quiet confidence of a well-engineered heating system humming invisibly behind perfectly plastered walls. Think of the emotional quality found in Scandinavian architectural tradition: buildings that honor their landscape rather than dominate it, interiors where every material is chosen for both beauty and function. Channel the atmosphere of a premium bathroom showroom where you can touch the fixtures, feel the tile textures, and imagine the warm water flowing — this is the sensory trust that Kruschel's Badausstellung in Neunkhausen offers, and the website should digitally echo that tactile, inviting experience. Reference the design philosophy of craftspeople who view sustainability not as a marketing buzzword but as an inherited responsibility — the kind of environmental consciousness that comes from generations living close to the Westerwald landscape. The color palette of aqua teal and fresh green should feel like water and forest, the two elements at the heart of what this company stewards. Let the overall design carry the quiet pride of a regional Meisterbetrieb: not flashy, not loud, but unmistakably excellent — like a perfectly heated home on a cold Westerwald winter evening.
