## Design-Prompt: infra-tec GmbH
**Style: Bauhaus**

Adopt a Bauhaus design philosophy to create a single-page website for infra-tec GmbH, a German steel infrastructure company specializing in noise protection walls, signal gantries, platform roof systems, rail web dampers, and structural engineering for the railway sector. The page should evoke a feeling of engineered precision and quiet industrial confidence — the same certainty one feels standing beside a perfectly welded steel beam that will endure decades of service. Visitors should sense structural integrity from the very first scroll: bold geometric sections anchored by infra-tec's steel-blue primary (#354B6A) and accented with their signature green (#3C763D) against a clean light background (#F6F6F6). Use colorful geometric elements — rectangles, circles, and clean angular dividers in these brand colors — to create rhythm and visual weight, mirroring the modular steel components infra-tec engineers every day. The visual hierarchy should feel like a blueprint come to life: each section a precisely placed structural element, the logo (https://infra-tec.de/wp-content/uploads/2018/10/infra_tec_logo_neu.svg) sitting confidently in the header like a builder's mark on finished steel.

The typographic system should communicate authority and engineering clarity using Rajdhani for headings — its geometric, slightly condensed letterforms echo technical drawings and specification sheets — paired with Rubik for body text, providing the warmth and readability needed to make complex infrastructure topics accessible. Interactions should feel mechanical and deliberate: scroll transitions that snap into place like steel modules locking together, hover states that reveal depth like layers of a structural cross-section. The emotional arc of the single-page journey should progress from awe (a full-bleed hero showcasing infra-tec's massive steel constructions along German railways) through trust (clearly structured service blocks for Lärmschutz, Signalausleger, Dachsysteme, Planung, and Schienenstegdämpfer, each with their real project photography) to conviction (a competence overview and news section that proves this company delivers — building under rolling wheels in the most demanding installation conditions). The call-to-action should feel inevitable, like the final bolt in a completed structure.

Draw inspiration from the emotional quality of Bauhaus architecture in Dessau — those buildings where every window placement, every material choice, every proportional relationship serves both function and beauty simultaneously. Channel the feeling of a perfectly organized German engineering workshop: tools precisely arranged, surfaces clean, every element purposeful yet aesthetically satisfying. Think of the quiet grandeur of a railway bridge at dawn — monumental steel geometry against soft morning light — and translate that duality of power and elegance into web design. Reference the mathematical beauty found in structural engineering diagrams, the satisfying grid of rivets on a steel plate, the way parallel railway tracks create perspective and draw the eye forward. These abstract qualities of industrial craftsmanship and geometric order should permeate every design decision, creating a page that feels as solidly constructed as infra-tec's own steel solutions for Germany's infrastructure.
