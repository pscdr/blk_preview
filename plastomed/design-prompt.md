## Design-Prompt: SEM-Plastomed
**Style: Swiss/International**

Adopt the Swiss/International design style to create a single-page website for SEM-Plastomed GmbH, a German manufacturer of precision plastic products for medical technology and technical applications, established in 1985. The page should radiate the quiet authority and systematic clarity that defines Swiss design — visitors arriving on this page should immediately feel they have entered the domain of meticulous engineering and uncompromising quality. The visual hierarchy must guide the eye with mathematical precision through SEM-Plastomed's two core divisions: medical technology (contract manufacturing, sterilization, packaging of medical components) and technical products (injection molding, tool creation, assembly). Use SEM-Plastomed's brand palette — a commanding crimson red (#BD0B30) as the primary accent against clean white backgrounds (#FFFFFF), with slate gray (#565F66) for body text and secondary elements — to create moments of controlled visual intensity within the grid. These red accents should feel like precise incisions of color: confident, deliberate, and energizing, reinforcing the surgical precision the company brings to its craft. Incorporate the company logo (https://www.plastomed.de/img/sem-plastomed.svg) and existing imagery from the original site to maintain brand continuity.

The typography should be set exclusively in Roboto Condensed — a condensed sans-serif that echoes the density and efficiency of Swiss typographic tradition. Headings in Roboto Condensed Bold should feel authoritative and engineered, like technical specifications etched into steel, while body text should read with effortless clarity, communicating complex manufacturing capabilities in clean, scannable blocks. The single-page journey should emotionally progress from confident introduction (the hero announcing innovative plastic solutions for medicine and technology) through trust-building evidence (divisions presented on a strict grid with images from https://www.plastomed.de/img/start/medizintechnik.jpg and https://www.plastomed.de/img/start/technische-produkte.jpg), to deeper credibility (since 1985, German manufacturing in Obererbach/Westerwald, values like flexibility, quality, and reliability), culminating in a clear call-to-action for production inquiries and contact. Interactions should feel snappy and precise — no unnecessary flourish, just purposeful micro-animations that reinforce the sense of mechanical accuracy. Scroll transitions should be clean cuts, not flowing dissolves.

Draw inspiration from the feeling of walking through a world-class precision manufacturing facility — the pristine order of a cleanroom, the satisfying geometry of injection molds arranged on racks, the confidence that comes from seeing decades of expertise reflected in every surface and system. Channel the aesthetic sensibility of mid-century Swiss poster design: the way Josef Müller-Brockmann balanced information density with visual calm, making complexity feel approachable through rigorous structure. Think of the emotional quality of German engineering — not cold or sterile, but warm in its competence, reassuring in its thoroughness. The page should feel like a precisely machined component: every element serves a function, every pixel has purpose, and the overall impression is one of quiet mastery. Reference the atmosphere of high-end medical environments where cleanliness meets human care, and technical exhibitions where innovation is presented with understated confidence. The final design should embody SEM-Plastomed's core promise — that behind every plastic component, every sterile package, every custom solution, stands a partner whose reliability is as structurally sound as the products they create.
