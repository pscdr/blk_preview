## Design-Prompt: Krüger Medienhaus
**Style: Swiss/International**

Adopt the Swiss/International typographic style to create a single-page landing page for Krüger Medienhaus, a heritage print house founded in 1947 in Hachenburg, Germany. This is a company whose craft IS typography and print — the design must honor that lineage with the disciplined clarity of the International Typographic Style. Visitors should feel immediate confidence: the kind of quiet authority that comes from a perfectly aligned grid, generous whitespace, and type set with absolute precision. The warm amber-gold (#FEA406) appears sparingly as an accent — a ruled underline, a highlighted number, a call-to-action — never decorative, always functional. The deep steel-blue (#446084) serves as the primary text and heading color, grounding everything with dependability. Backgrounds stay clean: white, off-white, and the lightest warm grays. No ornamentation, no flourishes — the grid is the design, and every element earns its place on it.

The design philosophy is rooted in the belief that clarity IS beauty. Typography in Lato should be set with careful attention to weight contrast — ultra-bold headings in large sizes create hierarchy through scale alone, while body text at comfortable reading sizes demonstrates the same care a master printer gives to leading and kerning. Interactions should feel precise and mechanical, like the clean snap of a paper cutter: sharp hover states, clean transitions, nothing bouncy or playful. The single-page journey should feel like walking through a well-organized production floor — each section clearly delineated, content blocks aligned to an invisible but palpable grid, generous breathing room between elements. The progression from hero through services, team, history, and contact should feel inevitable and logical, like a well-structured proposal document. Information hierarchy is everything: the visitor should never wonder where to look next.

Draw inspiration from the emotional quality of a freshly printed specimen sheet — the satisfaction of perfectly registered ink, the crispness of type on uncoated stock, the quiet pride of craftsmanship that needs no embellishment. Think of the functional beauty of a well-organized type case, where every character has its place and the system itself is elegant. Channel the philosophy of Swiss railway signage and Helvetica-era corporate identity: absolute legibility, democratic access to information, trust through consistency. The 77-year heritage and sustainability commitment of Krüger Medienhaus should feel embedded in the structure itself — not called out with badges or icons, but communicated through the reliability and precision of the design. The final page should feel like the company it represents: unpretentious, thoroughly competent, and built to last.
