## Design-Prompt: MAW Stahlbau Eulberg
**Style: Bauhaus**

Embrace the Bauhaus philosophy to create a single-page website for MAW-Stahlbau Eulberg GmbH & Co.KG, a steel construction and engineering firm from the Westerwald region with over 50 years of experience in structural steel halls, project construction, metal fabrication, and vessel/plant engineering. The page should radiate disciplined confidence and industrial gravitas — the feeling of walking into a perfectly organized workshop where every tool has its place and every beam is precisely measured. The visual hierarchy should channel the brand's steel-blue palette (#2991D6 as primary, #0089F7 as accent) against clean near-white backgrounds (#FCFCFC), creating a sense of structural clarity. Bold geometric shapes — rectangles, circles, and clean dividing lines — should organize content into distinct functional zones, echoing the precision of steel construction itself. As visitors arrive, they should feel they've entered the domain of serious craftspeople who build things that last. Incorporate colorful geometric accents sparingly to punctuate key sections and draw attention to the four core competencies: Stahl- und Hallenbau, Projektbau, Metallbau, and Behälter- und Anlagenbau.

The typography should feel authoritative yet approachable, using Poppins for body text to bring warmth and readability to technical content, while headings in a clean sans-serif communicate engineering precision. Every interaction should feel snappy and deliberate — like the clean click of a well-machined latch. Scroll transitions between sections should be crisp and mechanical, mirroring the exactness of steel fabrication. The single-page journey should emotionally progress from an immediate impression of scale and capability (the hero showing industrial work), through a methodical presentation of each service area — Projektbau's turnkey solutions, structural steel's flexibility, vessel engineering's technical depth, and metalwork's craftsmanship — building cumulative trust that this is a company that masters every dimension of steel. The narrative arc should move from "these people build impressive things" to "these people can build exactly what I need," culminating in a clear, confident call to action for contact. The logo (https://maw-ste.de/wp-content/uploads/2016/07/logo100.png) should anchor the header with understated permanence.

Think of the feeling inside a Bauhaus-era workshop — where raw materials meet intellectual rigor, where the beauty of a structure emerges from its honest function rather than applied decoration. Channel the emotional quality of exposed steel frameworks, the satisfying geometry of I-beams and trusses, the quiet pride of a precisely welded joint. Draw inspiration from the clarity of technical drawings, the organized power of an industrial hall, the confidence that comes from five decades of accumulated expertise. The design should feel like the architectural equivalent of a firm handshake — direct, competent, and trustworthy. Reference the emotional resonance of modernist industrial architecture, where materials speak for themselves and ornamentation gives way to structural truth. The final page should feel like a beautifully engineered object itself — every element load-bearing, nothing superfluous, the kind of design that makes an industrial client think: "These are the people I want building my next hall."
