# Design-Prompt: INNO FRICTION
**Style: Bauhaus**

Embrace the Bauhaus tradition — geometric simplicity, primary shapes, and the principle that form follows function — to create a single-page website for INNO FRICTION GmbH, Germany's specialist in friction materials since 1962. The page should evoke the feeling of walking into a precision engineering facility: controlled, confident, and quietly powerful. The deep petrol blue (#017299) serves as the dominant color identity, grounding every section with the weight of industrial authority while white space breathes clarity and openness into the layout. As visitors arrive, they should feel the intersection of heritage and innovation — the same tension that defines a company born from clutch lining manufacturing that now leads in e-mobility and renewable energy friction solutions. The visual hierarchy should guide the eye with the certainty of a well-calibrated machine: bold geometric headings in Roboto set against clean body text, circular and rectangular shapes framing the seven application areas (Automotive, Construction & Agricultural Machinery, Renewable Energy, Drive Technology, Rolling Mill Technology, Bearings, E-Mobility) as distinct visual modules. Introduce measured accent colors — a warm amber or industrial orange — sparingly against the petrol blue to create moments of visual friction that mirror the company's core expertise.

The design philosophy should channel Bauhaus's original intent: to unify art and industry, beauty and utility. Typography must feel engineered — Roboto in carefully weighted hierarchies that communicate both technical precision and approachability, where headings land with the authority of stamped metal and body text reads with the ease of a well-structured technical brief. Interactions should feel mechanical and satisfying — crisp transitions with the tactile quality of precision-machined components clicking into place, scroll-triggered reveals that unfold with the controlled energy of a hydraulic press. The emotional journey through this single page should progress from immediate impact (the "Der Reibbelagspezialist" hero statement establishing dominance) through growing respect (the breadth of applications, the COSID and Rex acquisitions showing strategic growth, the TOP 100 innovation award) to confident partnership (certifications like IATF 16949 and ISO 9001 as the final seal of trust). Each section transition should feel like moving through rooms in a Bauhaus exhibition — distinct yet unified by the same underlying grid logic and material honesty.

Think of the emotional quality found in the great industrial exhibition halls of the early 20th century — spaces where engineering achievements were presented with the reverence usually reserved for art. Channel the feeling of a Werkbund exhibition: every element purposeful, every proportion considered, every material honest about what it is. The design should carry the confidence of a century-old German engineering tradition meeting contemporary digital craft — like holding a perfectly machined metal component and feeling its weight, its smoothness, its intention. Draw from the emotional register of spaces where heavy industry meets intellectual rigor: the clean lines of a modernist factory floor, the systematic beauty of technical drawings, the satisfying geometry of interlocking mechanical parts. The INNO FRICTION logo — clean, precise, already Bauhaus-adjacent in its simplicity — should anchor the page as naturally as a cornerstone anchors a building. These references should produce a website that feels less like a corporate brochure and more like a curated presentation of industrial excellence — something that makes the viewer understand that behind these friction materials lies deep expertise, relentless innovation, and the kind of quiet confidence that comes from over sixty years of doing one thing exceptionally well.
