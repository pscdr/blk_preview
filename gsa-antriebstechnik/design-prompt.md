## Design-Prompt: G.S.A. Antriebstechnik
**Style: Modernist**

Adopt a Modernist design philosophy to craft a single-page landing page for G.S.A. Antriebstechnik, a German electrical drive technology company founded in 1995 and now led by a new generation. The page should evoke precision engineering, quiet confidence, and the calm authority of a company that has spent decades perfecting motion systems — electric motors, frequency inverters, 3D-printed components, and lean consulting. The emotional register upon arrival should be one of immediate trust: clean, purposeful, uncluttered. Use the brand's signature blue palette — a bright engineering blue (#49A4DC) as the primary accent against deep navy (#005387) headings — to establish technical credibility, while the light background (#F5F5F5) breathes openness and clarity. Introduce colorful moments sparingly: a warm accent or subtle gradient to humanize the industrial precision, ensuring the page feels alive rather than sterile. Set all typography in Nunito Sans, the brand's chosen typeface, leveraging its rounded geometry to soften the engineering rigor with approachability. The company logo (an SVG wordmark) should anchor the header with understated permanence. Display the original brand imagery — the hero background of industrial machinery, the product motor cutaway, the portrait of owner Adrian Vetten — as integral visual elements, not decoration.

The design philosophy should channel functionalism refined to its essence: every element earns its place through purpose. Typography should feel authoritative yet welcoming — Nunito Sans at generous sizes for headings creates confident statements, while body text at comfortable reading widths feels like a knowledgeable colleague explaining a solution. The visual hierarchy must guide the visitor through a narrative arc: the hero section delivers the brand promise ("Mehr als ein Motor — die Lösung für Bewegung") with cinematic scale; the three-pillar overview (Beratung, Entwicklung, Handel) establishes breadth through elegant iconography; the services section unfolds the 360° capability with structured clarity; the about section introduces the human story of generational continuity; and the contact section closes with inviting directness. Interactions should feel mechanically precise — smooth scroll transitions that echo the controlled motion of a well-engineered drive system. Hover states and micro-interactions should be subtle, purposeful, and satisfying, like the click of a precision instrument. The single-page journey should emotionally progress from "this company commands respect" through "they understand my exact problem" to "I want to work with them."

Draw inspiration from the emotional qualities of German industrial design halls — spaces where exposed steel meets polished concrete, where daylight floods through clerestory windows onto precisely arranged machinery. Think of the feeling inside a Bauhaus-era factory reimagined for the 21st century: the romance of engineering, the beauty of functional form, the quiet drama of things built to last. Channel the atmosphere of premium technical documentation — the kind of catalog where every photograph is perfectly lit, every diagram is exquisitely clear, and the paper stock itself communicates quality. Reference the design sensibility of world-class engineering exhibitions where complex systems are explained through elegant simplicity. The page should carry the emotional weight of craftsmanship passed between generations — the gravitas of 30 years of expertise meeting the fresh energy of new leadership under Adrian Vetten. These abstract qualities should permeate every pixel: the generous whitespace of confidence, the grid precision of engineering discipline, the typographic warmth of a company that sees itself not as a vendor but as a solution partner.
