## Design-Prompt: GERZ GmbH
**Style: Swiss/International**

Adopt the Swiss/International design tradition — disciplined grids, mathematical precision, and typography that commands authority through restraint — to reimagine the digital presence of GERZ GmbH, a German industrial manufacturer headquartered in Luckenbach specializing in innovative workspace solutions (industrial partition walls, welding protection screens, high-speed doors) and outdoor living products (Rollfenster®, terrace roofing systems). The single-page experience should evoke the quiet confidence of German engineering excellence: as visitors arrive, they should feel the weight of institutional reliability paired with forward-thinking innovation. The visual hierarchy must flow with the inevitability of a precision-machined component — each section locking into the next with grid-perfect alignment. Use GERZ's steel blue (#3A587D) as the commanding primary tone against clean white and warm grey (#F5F5F5) fields, introducing calculated moments of colorful accent — perhaps warm copper or industrial amber — to punctuate key conversion points and breathe warmth into the systematic structure. The GERZ logo (SVG vector) should anchor the composition with the permanence of a maker's mark stamped into steel.

The design philosophy must channel the Swiss principle that "form follows function" while creating a user experience that feels both intellectually satisfying and emotionally reassuring. Typography in Open Sans should be deployed with the discipline of a Müller-Brockmann grid — headlines at 48px carrying the authority of engineering specifications, body text at 16px with generous leading that allows ideas to breathe like precision-spaced components on a production line. Every interaction should feel mechanically precise yet human: scroll transitions that move with the smooth certainty of a hydraulic system, hover states that respond with the tactile click of quality hardware. The emotional arc of the single-page journey should progress from immediate industrial gravitas ("Vorsprung durch Innovation") through structured discovery of GERZ's dual expertise — workplace engineering and outdoor living — building methodically toward trust through their reference clients and manufacturing depth, culminating in a call-to-action that feels like the natural conclusion of a well-constructed argument. The zero border-radius design language reinforces the no-nonsense precision that defines both the Swiss style and GERZ's manufacturing identity.

Think of the feeling inside a world-class German manufacturing facility where every tool has its designated place and every surface gleams with maintained precision — this is the spatial quality the design must achieve. Channel the aesthetic philosophy of mid-century Swiss exhibition design: information presented with such clarity that complexity becomes accessible, where the grid itself becomes invisible yet everything feels inevitably, perfectly placed. Draw from the emotional register of Bauhaus-era industrial catalogs — documents that elevated functional products to objects of design admiration through sheer compositional discipline. The design should carry the gravitas of architectural blueprints, the trustworthiness of precision measurement instruments, and the quiet pride of a family business that has completed its succession planning and builds for generations. Every pixel should communicate what GERZ's tagline promises: advancement through innovation — not loud disruption, but the steady, systematic progress of a company that manufactures everything in-house at its German headquarters, where development, production, sales, and service truly come from a single hand.
