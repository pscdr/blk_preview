## Design-Prompt: SWW Stahlbau Westerwald
**Style: Swiss/International**

Adopt the Swiss/International design style to create a single-page website for SWW Stahlbau Westerwald, a steel construction and industrial hall building company based in Germany's Westerwald region. The page should radiate engineered precision, structural confidence, and quiet authority — the same qualities that define steel framework engineering itself. Visitors arriving at this page should immediately feel they are engaging with a company of deep technical competence and reliability. The visual hierarchy must guide the eye with the mathematical certainty of a well-calculated load-bearing structure: a bold hero section anchored by the deep navy primary color (#183252) establishes gravitas, while the vibrant construction-orange accent (#EC6912) punctuates key moments like call-to-action buttons and section highlights, creating energetic focal points against clean white (#FFFFFF) backgrounds. The SWW logo (wordmark in the company's established identity) sits with deliberate placement, and Titillium Web — the brand's chosen typeface — reinforces the technical, engineered character throughout all headings and body text. The emotional arc flows from immediate trust through demonstrated expertise to confident inquiry, with colorful accent elements marking the transition points between services like Hallenbau, Stahlbau, Korrosionsschutz, and Infrastrukturbau.

The design philosophy channels systematic grid-based thinking where every element earns its position through functional purpose, never decoration. Typography in Titillium Web should feel authoritative and technically precise at large heading sizes (bold, generous scale for the H1 "Stahlbau, der Zukunft formt"), transitioning to clear and readable at body size — always maintaining the disciplined rhythm of a well-set engineering document. Interactions should feel snappy and precise, like the click of precision-machined steel components locking into place — no excess motion, no ornamental flourish, just purposeful transitions that respect the user's time. The single-page journey emotionally progresses from an arresting hero statement (the company's core promise of "Alles aus einer Hand") through a systematic presentation of eight service areas displayed in a clean grid, each with its own icon-weight visual marker, down to a clear and compelling contact call-to-action. The scroll experience should feel like walking through a perfectly organized industrial facility: everything in its place, clearly labeled, immediately comprehensible, with the orange accent (#EC6912) serving as the wayfinding element that draws attention to the most important actions — "Unverbindlich anfragen."

Think of the aesthetic quality of a Swiss railway timetable — where absolute clarity becomes its own form of beauty. Channel the feeling of a precision-engineered factory floor where exposed steel beams create their own geometric pattern, or the confident simplicity of Bauhaus-adjacent industrial architecture where structure is celebrated rather than hidden. Reference the emotional quality of technical drawings rendered with care — where every line has purpose and the negative space between elements communicates as powerfully as the elements themselves. The deep navy (#183252) should evoke the weight and permanence of structural steel, while the orange (#EC6912) carries the energy of molten metal and the visibility of construction site safety markings. The design should feel like what happens when Swiss typographic discipline meets German engineering pride: restrained but never cold, systematic but never sterile, with the warmth coming through in the confident presentation of real expertise — decades of Stahlbau projects, hall constructions spanning 50+ meters, and the quiet pride of "Ihr Projekt ist bei uns in guten Händen."
