## Design-Prompt: Notar Dr. Stefan Braun
**Style: Editorial**

Create a sophisticated one-page website for Notar Dr. Stefan Braun, a German notary practice embodying the tagline "Persönlich. Neutral. Rechtssicher." (Personal. Neutral. Legally secure.) using an Editorial design style that evokes the refined authority of a premium legal publication. The emotional core should feel like opening a beautifully typeset law journal — immediate trust, quiet gravitas, and intellectual clarity. Visitors should sense they are in the hands of a meticulous professional the moment they arrive. The visual hierarchy should guide them through a flowing narrative: from a commanding hero statement through service philosophy, practical guidance, and current legal developments, each section feeling like a carefully curated editorial spread. Use a restrained palette anchored in deep black (#000000) and pure white (#FFFFFF) as the foundation, elevated by a single warm accent — a muted gold or aged brass tone — that introduces just enough warmth to transform formality into approachability. Typography is the hero: pair a refined serif like Playfair Display for headlines with a clean, highly legible sans-serif like Source Sans 3 for body text, honoring the practice's serif heritage while modernizing readability. Generous whitespace and considered colorful typographic details — pull quotes, oversized drop caps, fine ruled lines — should enhance rather than overwhelm.

The design philosophy centers on typography-driven storytelling where each scroll reveals a new editorial "page." Headlines should feel authoritative yet welcoming — the kind of confident calm that puts clients at ease during life's most significant legal moments: property purchases, marriage contracts, corporate formations, estate planning. Interactions should be smooth and measured, with subtle parallax on the header imagery and gentle fade-ins that feel like turning pages in a premium publication. The emotional arc progresses from gravitas (hero with the practice's credo) through reassurance (services and the "Beratung inklusive" philosophy — consultation included at no extra cost) to currency and competence (news section demonstrating active engagement with legal developments like electronic notarial documents). Micro-interactions — a fine underline growing beneath navigation links, a barely-perceptible shadow lifting a card on hover — should feel precise and intentional, reflecting the exactitude expected of notarial work. The single-page journey should culminate in a warm, personal call-to-action that echoes the "Persönlich" promise, making appointment booking feel like a natural next step rather than a transactional endpoint.

Draw inspiration from the atmosphere of a distinguished European law library — dark wood, brass reading lamps, the weight of vellum-bound volumes — translated into digital restraint. Channel the editorial confidence of premium broadsheet newspapers where every column, every margin, every caption placement reflects generations of refined craft. Think of the quiet authority found in neoclassical government architecture: symmetry that communicates stability, proportions that instill confidence, ornamentation that serves meaning rather than decoration. The Bauhaus principle of "less but better" should govern every decision — each element earning its place through function and beauty in equal measure. Reference the tactile quality of letterpress printing, where ink meets heavy cotton paper with purposeful impression, and let that sense of crafted permanence infuse the digital experience. The overall feeling should be that of a practice where tradition and modernity coexist seamlessly — where centuries-old legal principles are upheld with contemporary precision, and where every client interaction, like every typographic choice, is handled with care, competence, and quiet distinction.
