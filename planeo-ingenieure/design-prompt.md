## Design-Prompt: Planeo Ingenieure
**Style: Swiss/International**

Adopt the Swiss/International design style to create a single-page website for Planeo Ingenieure, a civil engineering and infrastructure planning firm based in Hachenburg, Germany, specializing in road construction, hydraulic engineering, urban planning, landscaping, sports facilities, construction management, and surveying — serving municipal clients since 2013. The page should evoke an immediate sense of systematic precision and quiet authority. As visitors arrive, they should feel the confidence of working with engineers who bring order to complex infrastructure challenges. The visual hierarchy must be rigorously grid-based, channeling the deep teal (#2E5B66) as the dominant accent against clean white backgrounds, with the cool grey (#666977) providing measured counterpoints in secondary text and dividers. The Planeo SVG logo anchors the top with understated permanence. Colorful imagery — construction sites, water infrastructure, surveyed landscapes — should be placed within strict grid cells, creating a rhythm of structured visual impact that mirrors the firm's disciplined approach to planning. Every element must breathe within generous whitespace, reinforcing the sense that this is a firm that plans with precision and leaves nothing to chance.

The typography must feel authoritative yet approachable, set entirely in Open Sans — headings at 48px for the hero and 32px for sections, body text at 16px — maintaining a single typeface system that speaks to engineering clarity. Interactions should be minimal and precise: subtle fade-ins on scroll, clean hover states on the seven service cards (Straßenbau, Wasserbau, Bauleitplanung, Freianlagen, Sportstätten, Bauleitung, Vermessung), and sharp geometric transitions that snap into place like well-drafted plans. The emotional arc should progress from an assured, panoramic hero introducing the three founders and their mission of sustainable infrastructure, through a methodical grid of service areas — each presented with real project photography and concise descriptions — building toward a clear, teal-accented call-to-action that invites contact. Buttons use zero border-radius, reinforcing the rectilinear discipline of the International style. The scrolling journey should feel like reviewing a meticulously prepared engineering proposal: organized, comprehensive, and confidence-inspiring.

Draw inspiration from the atmosphere of a well-organized architectural studio where every drawing is precisely pinned to the wall in perfect alignment — the kind of space where function and beauty are inseparable. Channel the ethos of mid-century Swiss graphic design: the belief that information, when presented with mathematical clarity, becomes inherently beautiful. Think of the feeling evoked by precise topographic maps, clean technical drawings, and the satisfying geometry of well-planned infrastructure — bridges, roads, and waterways that serve communities for decades. The design should carry the emotional quality of engineered permanence: structures built not for trends but for generations. Let the teal (#2E5B66) feel like deep, reliable water infrastructure; let the grey (#666977) feel like expertly laid road surfaces; let the white space feel like the careful planning that happens before ground is broken. The final page should embody the quiet pride of engineers who shape the physical landscape of the Westerwald region — professional, grounded, and built to last.
