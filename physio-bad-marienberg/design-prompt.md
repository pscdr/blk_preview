## Design-Prompt: PHYSIO Atelier
**Style: Organic/Fluid**

Adopt an Organic/Fluid design philosophy to craft a single-page website for PHYSIO Atelier, a physiotherapy practice led by Lena Melchert in Bad Marienberg, Germany. The page should feel like stepping into a space of healing and gentle movement — organic, flowing, and inherently calming. Use soft, curving shapes and fluid transitions between sections to mirror the body's natural movement patterns, reflecting the practice's core work in physiotherapy, children's therapy (Bobath), yoga, pilates, and rehabilitation sport. The brand's vivid magenta (#C7017F) should appear as an energizing accent — like a pulse of vitality — against warm white backgrounds, while the red (#FF0000) is used sparingly for calls-to-action. Integrate colorful, flowing gradient elements that transition between these brand tones, creating a sense of warmth and life throughout the page. The PHYSIO Atelier logo should be placed prominently, anchoring the organic visual flow.

The typographic system should feel both approachable and trustworthy: use Istok Web for headings to convey grounded professionalism, Exo 2 for display moments that add a subtle modern edge, and Roboto for body text that reads effortlessly. Headlines should feel like a calm, confident invitation — never clinical or cold. Interactions and scroll-based transitions should mimic organic motion: sections flowing into one another like breath — inhale, pause, exhale — creating a rhythm that mirrors the therapeutic experience itself. The emotional journey progresses from an immediate sense of welcome and trust at the top (the team's competence and care), through discovery of the diverse therapy and course offerings (pilates, yoga, rehasport, babymassage), toward a warm, clear call-to-action at the bottom that feels like a natural next step rather than a push. Every micro-interaction should feel gentle and fluid, never abrupt.

Think of the feeling of a sunlit wellness studio with large windows and natural light — spaces where curved wooden architecture meets soft textiles. Draw from the emotional qualities of biophilic design principles, where organic forms in architecture create environments that reduce stress and promote healing. Channel the sophistication of contemporary Scandinavian spa interiors — understated luxury through natural materials and flowing spatial design. Reference the visual language of kinetic art installations where movement creates calm rather than chaos, and the gentle precision of Japanese garden design where every curve is intentional yet appears effortless. These abstract reference points should inform a design that communicates PHYSIO Atelier's dual identity: medical competence and human warmth, professional precision and organic care — a digital space that feels as therapeutic as the treatments within.
