## Design-Prompt: Schäfer Haustechnik
**Style: Industrial Craft**

Envision a website for Schäfer Haustechnik GmbH & Co. KG — a regional home services company uniting three trades (electrical, heating, and sanitary) under one roof — that channels the confident, grounded aesthetic of industrial craftsmanship refined for the digital age. The visual language should evoke the feeling of walking into a meticulously organized master workshop: every tool has its place, every surface is purposeful, every detail speaks to decades of accumulated expertise. The primary palette anchors around a trustworthy steel blue (#337AB7) that recalls technical precision and reliability, punctuated by a bold signal red (#E2001A) used sparingly for calls-to-action and critical emphasis — like the red markings on professional-grade tools. The background remains clean white (#FFFFFF), creating breathing room that lets the craftsmanship speak. Headlines set in Raleway carry geometric clarity with just enough character to feel human, while Roboto body text ensures effortless readability across all technical descriptions of electrical systems, heating installations, and sanitary renovations.

The design philosophy treats the three-trade advantage as the architectural spine of the entire experience. Imagine a layout where each service area — Elektro, Heizung, Sanitär — is presented as a distinct material panel, almost like specimens in a curated exhibition of building trades, yet unified by consistent grid structure and shared design DNA. Navigation should feel as intuitive as a well-labeled breaker panel: immediately clear, never ambiguous. Interactive elements use the warm charcoal (#4B453F) of seasoned workbenches for buttons and UI anchors, grounding the digital interface in physical materiality. Micro-interactions should feel mechanical and satisfying — toggles that click, sections that slide with weight, hover states that reveal depth like opening a technical drawing. The overall UX cadence is unhurried but efficient, mirroring how a seasoned Meisterbetrieb approaches a project: thorough assessment, clear communication, precise execution. The phone number 02741/22418 and the weekday availability (7:30–16:00) should be ever-present, not hidden — accessibility is a core brand promise, and the design must honor it.

Think of this design as the digital equivalent of a precision-engineered German tool catalog crossed with the warmth of a neighborhood craftsman's storefront. Reference points include: the systematic beauty of Dieter Rams' product design philosophy applied to a service business; the material honesty of exposed copper pipes and clean cable runs elevated to visual motif; the confidence of a Handwerksmeister's certificate displayed on an office wall — authoritative without being pretentious. The logo (http://www.xn--schfer-haustechnik-ntb.de/wp-content/uploads/2018/09/Schaefer_Start_Logo.jpg) should sit prominently like a quality seal, reinforcing the GmbH & Co. KG's established presence in the region. Hero imagery of electrical work, heating systems, and sanitary installations provides authentic texture, while the partner manufacturer logos serve as trust badges — quality associations that validate the company's commitment to premium materials. Every pixel should communicate: "We are three skilled trades, one reliable team, one phone call away."
