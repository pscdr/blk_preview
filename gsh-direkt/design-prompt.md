## Design-Prompt: Gebrüder Schneider GmbH (GSH Direkt)
**Style: Swiss/International**

Adopt the Swiss/International design style to craft a single-page website for Gebrüder Schneider GmbH — a precision toolmaking and electronic specialty manufacturer with over 100 years of heritage, serving the automotive sector, toolmaking (Werkzeugbau), gauge construction (Lehrenbau), and specialized lighting. The page should feel like stepping into a meticulously organized workshop where every element has its exact place. As visitors arrive, they should experience immediate confidence and trust — the deep navy (#002C73) of the text and the strong corporate blue (#2663BE) should anchor a visual hierarchy that feels authoritative yet approachable. The grid must be absolute, the rhythm mathematical, and every section should breathe with generous whitespace against the soft #F8F8F8 background. Use the accent green (#338800) sparingly but deliberately — like a precision instrument's indicator light — to draw the eye to calls-to-action and key differentiators. The GSH logo (https://www.gsh-direkt.de/template/GSH/pix/logo.png) should sit with quiet confidence at the top, never oversized, always perfectly aligned. Incorporate the existing imagery — the hero slider visuals and the welcoming company photograph — as documentary-style anchors that ground the abstract precision of the layout in real craftsmanship.

The typography must feel engineered: Nunito Sans as the sole typeface family, used across headings and body text, leveraging weight contrast rather than font variety to create hierarchy — bold condensed headings at 40px that command attention, regular body text at 19px that reads effortlessly. Every line of text should feel like it was placed with calipers. Interactions should be crisp and mechanical — hover states that shift with Swiss-clock precision, scroll transitions that are smooth but never indulgent, micro-animations that suggest the reliable click of a well-made tool. The emotional journey should progress from awe (hero section: century of innovation, precision imagery) through understanding (four specialties presented in a perfect grid of cards) to trust (company description with documentary photography) and finally to action — each section separated by mathematical spacing, each transition deliberate. The single-page flow should feel like reading a beautifully typeset technical manual where every element serves a function and nothing decorative exists without purpose. Buttons should be sharp-cornered (#338800 green on primary, clean and no-nonsense) reflecting the zero-tolerance precision of Schneider's craft.

The aesthetic should channel the feeling of a pristine German engineering floor — the organized calm of a Bauhaus-era factory, the intellectual rigor of a Zurich design studio in the 1960s, the satisfying order of Swiss railway timetables and instrument panels. Think of the emotional quality found in precision measurement tools: the confidence of exact tolerances, the beauty of functional perfection, the quiet pride of a century-old firm that has never stopped refining its craft. The cultural DNA of International Typographic Style — where clarity is beauty and the grid is sacred — should inform every decision, from the column structure to the padding ratios. Reference the atmosphere of industrial heritage museums where old machinery is displayed with reverence, where craftsmanship becomes art. The final design should feel like the digital equivalent of a perfectly machined component: flawless surfaces, exact proportions, no wasted material — a website that Gebrüder Schneider themselves would recognize as sharing their values of quality, precision, and enduring innovation.
