## Design-Prompt: WERIT
**Style: Bauhaus**

Adopt a Bauhaus design philosophy to create a single-page landing page for WERIT Kunststoffwerke — a European industrial plastics manufacturer with 75 years of heritage, producing IBC containers, plastic pallets, transport packaging, building technology, and sustainable water systems across ten locations. The page should evoke a feeling of engineered precision meeting purposeful warmth: visitors should sense immediately that they are engaging with a company where every form serves a function, where raw industrial strength is refined into geometric elegance. The visual hierarchy must flow with the confidence of a factory floor — structured, rhythmic, and deeply intentional. WERIT's bold orange (#FF5C00) should act as the kinetic accent cutting through clean whites and measured dark grays (#3B3B3B), creating focal points with the same decisiveness as a Bauhaus primary color study. Geometric blocks and shapes should frame content areas, giving the page an architectural quality where negative space is as deliberate as the content it surrounds. Every element — from the WERIT wordmark to product imagery of containers, pallets, and water systems — should feel placed on an invisible grid with mathematical certainty. Incorporate colorful geometric accents that reference the energy and optimism of WERIT's "value-driven + future ready" positioning.

The typography must feel authoritative yet approachable — Open Sans rendered with generous spacing and bold weights for headlines that command attention, paired with lighter weights for body text that invites reading. Text should feel like it was set with the same care as a Bauhaus exhibition poster: every line length considered, every hierarchy level earning its visual distinction. Interactions should feel precise and mechanical — clean state transitions, sharp hover effects, elements that snap into place rather than float. The single-page scroll journey should progress like walking through a modern German production facility: entering through a commanding hero statement ("WERIT startet durch"), moving through clearly delineated product zones (Industrieverpackungen, Haustechnik, Spezialprodukte), passing the brand story with its 75-year heritage, encountering the sustainability commitment ("ReUse. ReThink. ReSource."), the industry sectors served, quality certifications, and arriving at a confident call-to-action. Each section transition should feel like stepping through a perfectly engineered doorway — defined, clean, satisfying.

Think of the feeling inside a Bauhaus building in Dessau — that extraordinary sensation of light, geometry, and purpose working in absolute harmony. Channel the spirit of industrial design museums where massive manufactured objects are displayed with gallery-level reverence, where a shipping container becomes sculpture and a plastic pallet becomes a study in structural efficiency. Reference the emotional quality of German engineering exhibitions, the crisp authority of Swiss rail station signage, the satisfying logic of modular building systems. The page should carry the DNA of movements that believed functional objects deserve beauty — that a factory can be a cathedral, that a product catalog can be art. WERIT's identity as a family-owned company ("Wir denken nicht in Quartalen, sondern in Generationen") should resonate through design choices that feel timeless rather than trendy, built to last rather than built to impress momentarily. The final design should make industrial plastics feel not just important, but genuinely compelling — transforming utilitarian products into objects of considered design.
