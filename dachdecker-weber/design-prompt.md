# Design-Prompt: Dachdecker Weber
**Style: Swiss/International**

Employing the Swiss/International design style, create a single-page website for Dachdecker Weber GmbH — a family-owned roofing and building services company with over 50 years of heritage in quality craftsmanship. The page should evoke an immediate sense of precision, trustworthiness, and deep-rooted expertise. As visitors arrive, they should feel the confidence that comes from encountering a meticulously organized, grid-perfect layout where every element has earned its place. The visual hierarchy should guide them with the calm authority of a master craftsman presenting their finest work. The color palette anchors itself in a distinguished steel blue (#386992) as the primary tone — evoking slate rooftops and dependable skies — punctuated by a bold signal red (#F53100) that commands attention for calls-to-action and key highlights. Use these colors with Swiss restraint: the blue as a foundational element of trust, the red as precise moments of energy and urgency. The white background (#FFFFFF) must breathe generously, giving each content block the gravity and presence it deserves.

The typographic system is the backbone of the entire experience. Archivo Black for headings delivers a bold, industrial authority — each headline should feel carved in stone, echoing the permanence of the roofs Weber builds. Poppins as the body typeface provides approachable warmth and excellent readability, creating a dialogue between strength and accessibility. The design philosophy channels the original Swiss school: a rigorous underlying grid that creates order from complexity, asymmetric but balanced compositions, and generous negative space that signals confidence rather than emptiness. The single-page journey should progress emotionally from an authoritative first impression (50 years of mastery), through a structured presentation of three core services — Bedachung, Fassaden, Isolierung — each given equal visual weight in a systematic layout, to a trust-building partner section, and finally to a clear, compelling call-to-action. Interactions should feel precise and mechanical — no frivolity, just purposeful movement that mirrors the exactness of expert craftsmanship.

Draw inspiration from the emotional qualities of mid-century Swiss railway stations — spaces where engineering precision meets human comfort, where signage systems guide effortlessly, and where materials speak of permanence and civic pride. Channel the feeling of walking through a perfectly organized workshop where every tool has its designated place, where decades of accumulated expertise are visible in the worn-smooth handles and impeccably maintained equipment. Think of the quiet confidence of post-war German industrial design — the Werkbund philosophy that beauty emerges naturally from perfect function. The page should feel like opening a premium technical catalog from a master guild: authoritative typographic hierarchies, photographic content presented in clean geometric frames, and an overall composition that communicates "we have been doing this longer than anyone, and we do it better." The partner logos (Braas, Vedag, Icopal, Velux, Roto) should be presented as a curated gallery of professional alliances, reinforcing the systematic, quality-driven worldview of the brand.
