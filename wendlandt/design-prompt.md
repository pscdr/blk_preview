# Design-Prompt: Wendlandt
**Style: Swiss/International**

Adopt the Swiss/International design style to craft a single-page website for Wendlandt Unternehmensberatung, a venerable management consultancy specializing in public transit and bus companies since 1976. The page should radiate quiet authority and institutional confidence — the feeling of entering a well-ordered office where every detail has been considered with Germanic precision. Use the brand's deep forest green (#236621) as the dominant anchor, supported by the fresh, forward-looking accent green (#78B819) to signal vitality within tradition. Set all typography in Roboto, leveraging its geometric neutrality to echo the Swiss ethos of clarity above ornamentation. The visual hierarchy should guide the visitor with the calm certainty of a railway timetable: from a commanding hero headline ("Führend in der Verkehrsberatung — Gemeinsam zu neuen Zielen") through structured service grids to a decisive call-to-action, each section separated by generous whitespace and ruled by an underlying twelve-column grid. The Wendlandt raven logo (https://www.wendlandt.de/images/wendlandt_Logo_mit_Rabe_neu.jpg) should anchor the top-left with understated permanence. Introduce colorful accents sparingly — the lime green (#78B819) for hover states, buttons, and data highlights — so that every splash of color carries informational weight rather than decoration.

The design philosophy must feel like reading a beautifully typeset annual report from a firm you would trust with your company's future. Typography should be authoritative yet approachable: bold Roboto headings at generous scale (38px+ for h1, 36px for h2) commanding attention without shouting, body text at 18px providing effortless readability. Interactions should be precise and understated — micro-animations that feel like the satisfying click of a well-machined mechanism: subtle fade-ins on scroll, buttons with crisp state transitions, service cards that lift with restrained shadows. The single-page journey should emotionally progress from gravitas (hero: 50 years of expertise) through competence (13 consulting services laid out in a clean, scannable grid) to trust (BDU membership, industry-specific knowledge) and finally to invitation (contact form and CTA). The overall UX should feel like the consulting experience itself: structured, expert-led, and reassuringly methodical. The light background (#E3E3E3 softened toward white) ensures the deep green typography and accents project with maximum contrast and readability, while rounded primary buttons (30px radius) provide the singular moment of softness in an otherwise disciplined system.

Draw inspiration from the emotional quality of mid-century Swiss railway station design — those spaces where Helvetica signage, precise scheduling displays, and immaculate wayfinding created an atmosphere of absolute reliability. Channel the feeling of a well-curated corporate archive: leather-bound precision, the satisfying alignment of a perfectly set table of contents, the confidence that comes from decades of accumulated expertise organized into accessible, navigable systems. Think of the refined restraint found in institutional design from Northern European capitals — where civic buildings communicate authority through proportion and material honesty rather than excess. The BDU (Bundesverband Deutscher Unternehmensberatungen) affiliation should feel like a quality seal naturally embedded in the design's DNA, not appended as an afterthought. Let these abstract references — the timetable's certainty, the archive's depth, the institution's permanence — infuse every pixel of the final design, creating a digital presence that feels as established and trustworthy as Wendlandt's five decades of service to Germany's transportation sector.
