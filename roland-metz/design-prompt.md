## Design-Prompt: Roland Metz
**Style: Scandinavian (hygge, natural materials, warm minimalism)**

Create a one-page landing page for Roland Metz Malermeister, a master painter and interior finishing specialist in the Westerwald region of Germany, offering Trendputz (lime plaster finishes), designer wallpapers, facade work, flooring, LED lighting integration, and classic painting and lacquering services. The page should embody the Scandinavian design ethos — visitors should feel as though they've stepped into a thoughtfully crafted living space where natural warmth and quiet confidence coexist. The emotional arrival should communicate trust, craftsmanship, and the subtle pleasure of beautifully finished surfaces. As they scroll through this single cohesive page, the visual hierarchy should feel like moving through rooms of a perfectly renovated home — each section revealing a new layer of expertise without ever feeling cluttered or hurried. The color palette anchors on the brand's slate-grey primary (#4A525F) as a grounding tone, with the vibrant lime-green accent (#BFD72F) appearing as moments of fresh energy — like spring light entering a carefully designed interior. Use the Roland Metz logo (https://rolandmetz.de/wp-content/uploads/2016/10/logo_rolandmetz.png) prominently, and set typography in Roboto for headings and Open Sans for body text to maintain a clean, approachable professionalism. Integrate colorful elements through the accent green on calls-to-action and interactive highlights, creating pops of vitality against the warm, restrained palette. Light backgrounds throughout — think warm whites and soft greys — ensuring the page breathes with Scandinavian openness.

The design philosophy should channel warmth through restraint. Typography should feel welcoming yet authoritative — Roboto headings rendered with generous weight convey the confidence of a Malermeister (master craftsman), while Open Sans body text reads with the easy approachability of a trusted advisor walking you through material samples. Interactions and hover states should feel gentle and organic — soft transitions, subtle lifts, elements that respond like a hand running across a freshly plastered wall. The emotional arc of the single-page journey should progress from the reassuring confidence of the hero (featuring Roland Metz's tagline "Ihr Spezialist im Westerwald" and the service telephone 0151 212 65 333) through the quiet authority of the service sections (Trendputz, Tapeten, Fassade, Boden, LED-Lichtgestaltung, Malerarbeiten), into the personal warmth of the Über-uns section with Roland Metz's portrait and philosophy, and finally into an inviting contact section that feels like an open door rather than a form. The lime-green (#BFD72F) CTAs should feel like natural moments of invitation — never aggressive, always warm — echoing the brand's personality of professional craftsmanship with genuine passion. Each section should have room to breathe, with generous whitespace that mirrors the calm satisfaction of a freshly finished room.

Think of the feeling of entering a Scandinavian design showroom where every material has been chosen with intention — the smooth coolness of lime plaster walls, the warmth of natural wood flooring underfoot, the soft glow of integrated LED lighting at dusk. Channel the aesthetic sensibility of Nordic craft traditions where the maker's hand is always present but never ostentatious — the quiet pride of a workshop where tools are arranged with care and every surface tells a story of skilled labor. Draw from the emotional quality of hygge — that untranslatable sense of cozy contentment — translated into a digital experience: sections that feel like intimate rooms rather than billboards, imagery that invites you to touch surfaces and imagine them in your own home. Reference the refined simplicity of Scandinavian architecture where structural honesty meets organic materials, where light is celebrated as a design element, and where the boundary between interior and nature softens. The Westerwald region's rolling landscape and artisanal heritage should subtly inform the design's groundedness — this is not metropolitan minimalism but the warm, rooted minimalism of a craftsman who knows his materials intimately. These abstract qualities should permeate every pixel, creating a digital space that feels as carefully finished as the walls and floors Roland Metz creates for his clients.
